Brand

Brand as a strategic device

 Brands are an important instrument for managing firms’ markets

 Identifies the associations consumers should make in relation to a product

  A brand identifies for consumers who the product is meant for by engendering associations that are appealing to certain consumers. Thus, the brand actually specifies the segment(s) of the target market for which the branded product is meant

 Differentiates the product from competitor products

  A brand positions a product vis-a-vie its competitors. Thus, the brand articulates the differential advantage of the product.

Value of the brand

 the value of the brand is stored in the buyers mind - Share of mind It is an asset because it has a lasting effect on the company and is not consumed by the first user

How do brands add value beyond differentiating a product through meaning?

 Through its ability to reduce risk (for buyer)

  Quality risk

  Identity risk

  Time risk

  Promise of pact (Trust risk)

  simplification of product decisions

(Quality risk, Identity risk, Time risk, Promise of pact (Trust risk))

 Also its ability to reduce risk (for sellers)

  Demand risk

  Competitor risk

  Efficiency risk

  Legal risk

  Communication risk

Purpose (the strategic intent)

 To create a perceived difference among products

Objective

 To add value to the physical product or service

How is the value of a brand defined?

 brand's price

  the value of strategic market position for the brand owner in financial terms.

 brand's equity

  the value of the brand's strategic market position for the brand owner in terms of the brand's differential effect on consumer choices.
